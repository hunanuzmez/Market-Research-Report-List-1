<p><p>Microturbine generators are compact power generation systems that convert various fuels into electricity. The market is divided into two primary types: unrecuperated microturbines and recuperated microturbines. Unrecuperated microturbines operate without a heat recovery system, offering lower efficiency but simpler design and lower costs. In contrast, recuperated microturbines incorporate heat recovery to enhance efficiency by reusing exhaust heat, making them more suitable for applications requiring higher output and reduced emissions. Both types cater to different energy needs and operational preferences.</p></p>
