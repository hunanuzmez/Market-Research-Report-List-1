<p><ul><li>PPS (Polyphenylene Sulfide)</li><li>PBT (Polybutylene Terephthalate)</li><li>PA (Polyamide)</li><li>PC (Polycarbonate)</li><li>PEI (Polyethylenimine)</li><li>PSU (Polysulfone)</li><li>PEEK (Polyether Ether Ketone)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The thermally conducting polymer market comprises various types, each offering unique thermal and mechanical properties. PPS provides excellent chemical resistance and thermal stability, while PBT is known for its electrical insulating properties and heat resistance. PA, or polyamide, offers durability and flexibility, whereas PC, or polycarbonate, is valued for its impact resistance and transparency. PEI is recognized for high thermal stability. PSU provides strong thermal and mechanical performance. PEEK, a high-performance polymer, offers superior thermal resistance. Other polymers in this category include blends and specialized formulations tailored to specific applications.</p></p>
