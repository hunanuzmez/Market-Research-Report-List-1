<p><p>The plastic cards market comprises primarily two types: smart cards and regular cards. Smart cards are embedded with electronic chips, allowing for secure transactions, data storage, and advanced functionalities like contactless payments. They are commonly used in banking, access control, and identification. Regular cards, typically magnetic stripe cards, are simpler in design and rely on magnetic strips for data. While they are widely used for payments and loyalty programs, they lack the enhanced security features of smart cards, making them less secure.</p></p>
