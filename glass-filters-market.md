Colorless glass filters are designed to transmit light without altering its hue, often used in optical applications, photography, and scientific instruments. In contrast, colored glass filters selectively absorb certain wavelengths of light, enhancing or modifying color for artistic, imaging, or industrial purposes. Both types play crucial roles in various sectors, including photography, medical diagnostics, and lighting design, catering to diverse aesthetic and functional needs.</p></p>
