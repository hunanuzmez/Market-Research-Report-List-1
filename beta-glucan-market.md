<p><p>The beta-glucan market is primarily categorized into soluble and insoluble types. Soluble beta-glucan, often derived from sources like oats and barley, is renowned for its health benefits, including cholesterol reduction and immune system support. It is commonly used in functional foods and dietary supplements. In contrast, insoluble beta-glucan, typically extracted from certain fungi and yeast, primarily serves structural and textural roles in products. Both types cater to distinct applications in food, pharmaceuticals, and nutraceuticals, addressing diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
