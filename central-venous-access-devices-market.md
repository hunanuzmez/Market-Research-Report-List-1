<p><p>The Central Venous Access Devices market encompasses various types, including peripheral intravenous (PIV) catheters and midline catheters. PIV catheters are short-term devices inserted into peripheral veins for administering medications and fluids. Midline catheters are longer, inserted into peripheral veins, and suitable for intermediate therapy lasting up to several weeks. Other devices in this market include tunneled catheters and implantable ports, which offer options for long-term access and are used in patients requiring ongoing treatment or chemotherapy.</p></p>
