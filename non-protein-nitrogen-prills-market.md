<p><p>Non-Protein Nitrogen Prills encompass various types, primarily Urea, Ammonia, and Biuret. Urea, widely used in fertilizers, provides a high nitrogen content, enhancing plant growth. Ammonia, in its gaseous or aqueous form, serves as a crucial nitrogen source for manufacturing fertilizers and offers quick nutrient availability. Biuret, a derivative of urea, contributes to slow-release nitrogen, minimizing losses and providing sustained nutrition to plants. These forms play essential roles in agronomy, affecting crop yields and soil health.</p></p>
