<p><p>The preclinical imaging market includes diverse systems that facilitate biomedical research. Optical imaging systems utilize light to visualize biological processes, while nuclear imaging systems detect gamma radiation from radioisotopes. Micro-MRI systems provide high-resolution images of small animal anatomy, and micro-ultrasound systems offer real-time imaging for dynamic studies. Micro-CT systems allow for 3D imaging of small structures, whereas preclinical photoacoustic imaging combines light and sound for functional imaging. Lastly, magnetic particle imaging (MPI) systems enable real-time imaging using superparamagnetic nanoparticles, enhancing visualization in preclinical studies.</p></p>
