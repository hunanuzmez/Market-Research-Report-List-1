<p><p>The Styrene Monomer (SM) market predominantly features two production methods: Ethylbenzene Dehydrogenation and Ethylbenzene Oxidation. The Ethylbenzene Dehydrogenation method involves the removal of hydrogen from ethylbenzene to produce styrene, known for its efficiency and lower energy consumption. In contrast, the Ethylbenzene Oxidation method converts ethylbenzene into styrene through an oxidation process, offering advantages like high selectivity. Both methods are critical in catering to the rising demand for styrene in industries such as plastics, automotive, and packaging.</p></p>
