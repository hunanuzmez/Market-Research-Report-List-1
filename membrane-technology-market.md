<p><p>The membrane technology market encompasses various filtration processes, including ultrafiltration, microfiltration, and nanofiltration, each serving distinct applications. Microfiltration employs membranes with larger pore sizes to separate suspended solids and larger microorganisms, typically used in water treatment. Ultrafiltration further reduces particle size, targeting smaller pathogens and colloids, essential for food processing and pharmaceuticals. Nanofiltration, with even smaller pores, selectively removes divalent ions and small organic molecules, often applied in water softening and wastewater treatment, catering to specific separation needs across industries.</p></p>
